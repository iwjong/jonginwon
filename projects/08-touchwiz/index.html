TouchWiz 3.0 was developed as the interface foundation for Samsung's Bada platform (WAVE), and the work required close coordination across visual and interaction design. I contributed as part of a five-designer team to define an interface system that could scale across future Samsung devices.

I was early in my career then, so much of the work came through hands-on making and learning constraints in real time. As lead designer for the TouchWiz UX direction, I shaped key visual and interaction patterns later applied across the Galaxy S and Galaxy Note lines. The focus was on clearer navigation and more usable app organization so people could search, arrange, and manage content with less friction.

With Galaxy S3, I refined the home experience further through updates to navigation, edit mode, and widget behavior. The result was a more fluid and productive interface that improved day-to-day usability while keeping the system coherent across devices. Looking back, the project taught me as much about process discipline as final output.
